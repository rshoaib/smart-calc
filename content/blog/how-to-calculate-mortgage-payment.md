## 1. The Real Cost of a Mortgage: Understanding PITI

When most people think of a mortgage payment, they only think of the loan itself. But a true monthly payment consists of four distinct parts, collectively known as **PITI**:

1. **Principal:** The portion of your payment that goes toward paying down the actual balance of the loan.
2. **Interest:** The cost of borrowing the money, paid to the lender.
3. **Taxes:** Property taxes collected by your local government. Lenders usually collect this monthly and hold it in an escrow account.
4. **Insurance:** Homeowners insurance (to protect the property) and, if your down payment is less than 20%, Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) to protect the lender.

If you don't factor in taxes and insurance, your estimated payment will be significantly lower than the reality. That's why using a comprehensive [mortgage calculator](/finance/mortgage) is critical.

## 2. The Mortgage Calculation Formula

If you want to calculate the Principal and Interest (P&I) by hand, there is a standard mathematical formula used by every bank in the world.

Here is the formula:

**M = P [ r(1 + r)^n ] / [ (1 + r)^n - 1 ]**

Where:
- **M** = Total monthly payment (Principal + Interest)
- **P** = Principal loan amount (Home price minus down payment)
- **r** = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate divided by 12)
- **n** = Number of monthly payments (For a 30-year loan, n = 360)

### Let's Look at an Example

Suppose you are buying a **$400,000** home with a **20% down payment** ($80,000). 
Your principal loan amount (P) is **$320,000**.
Your interest rate is **6.5%**.

- **r** = 0.065 ÷ 12 = **0.005416**
- **n** = 30 years × 12 months = **360**

Plug those into the formula, and your Principal & Interest payment comes out to roughly **$2,022 per month**.

But remember, this is *just* the P&I. You still need to add Property Taxes, Homeowners Insurance, and potentially HOA fees to find your true monthly cost.

## 3. How Different Factors Impact Your Payment

Your monthly payment isn't static. It moves drastically depending on the levers you pull during the financing process. 

### The Down Payment
Putting down 20% is the golden rule because it eliminates PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance). If you put down 5% instead, your principal loan amount is larger, meaning your base payment is higher, *and* you will have an extra $100–$300 PMI charge tacked onto your monthly bill.

### The Interest Rate
Even a 1% difference in your interest rate can dramatically change your affordability.
On a $300,000 loan:
- At **5.5%**, your P&I is ~$1,703.
- At **6.5%**, your P&I is ~$1,896.
- That 1% difference costs you an extra **$200 a month**, or nearly **$70,000** over the life of the loan.

### The Loan Term
- **30-Year Fixed:** The standard. Lower monthly payments, but you pay significantly more total interest over 30 years.
- **15-Year Fixed:** Higher monthly payments, but a much lower interest rate and massive savings on total interest paid.
